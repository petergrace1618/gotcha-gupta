Praefatio

Gyps Amanda Melissa Ruiz (hereinafter G) and I lived together from March 2023 to November 2024.

In late August 2024, she went on a trip out of state, and came back broke and unemployed; I paid her rent and bills in September and October.

On November 2 2024, she said in several text messages and a voicemail that she gave away some of my stuff.

On Monday November 4 2024 when I moved out, the following things were missing:

  • Three of my guitars
  • My guitar amp
  • My comic book collection
  • Two guitar stands
  • A collection of about 60 Crown Royal bags, and
  • A bag of kitchen utensils

A week later she denied it.

Six weeks after that I found out she slandered me, so I decided to sue her in small claims court.

DISCLAIMER

This is not defamation, nor doxing, but counterspeech.

All statements I've made about her are true and factual to the best of my knowledge.

All personal information contained herein have been redacted; all else is in the public record except those made by a third party which I presume to be true but may be considered hearsay.

If she wants to tell lies about me, then I'll tell the truth about her.

Some fundamentals first.

Postulate
People generally tell the truth, and assume that others generally tell the truth.
Corollary
If a charming and charismatic girl tells a horror story about her psycho abusive ex-boyfriend, people will most likely believe it.
Caveat
Unless presented with evidence to the contrary.

Well, here's some evidence to the contrary.

Indicia Contraria

(1)

In April 2025, I found out she told someone I know that she sued me in small claims court which is laughably absurd because it's the easiest to refute, and also ironic because it was the same day the judgment was entered against her.

  1. These court documents clearly show who sued whom.
    • SMALL CLAIM AND NOTICE OF SMALL CLAIM
    • CERTIFICATE OF SERVICE
    • NOTICE OF ENTRY OF JUDGMENT
  2. An Online Records Search for “Ruiz, Amanda Gyps” on the Oregon Judicial Department website also shows this.
  3. This letter from a producer of the nationally syndicated court TV show called "Hot Bench" also shows this.

In fact, she had two opportunities to have her day in court but she ignored both. Probably because she knew that her whole defense is so full of holes and is so easily refutable that she would have lost in court and/or looked bad on TV.

You be the judge. This is my case against her.
(2)

She called me on December 27 2024 under the pretense of checking on my well-being. During the call I told her that her text messages are part of a police report. She said she filed a police report, too.

  • This document from the Portland Police Bureau proves otherwise. The “Related Event(s)” section shows there's no record of her filing a police report between the date of the incident and the date she called. (For the sake of transparency, here is my police report.)

Her call and the events leading up to it perfectly exemplify her disingenuous behavior.

(3)

In early March 2023, her ex-boyfriend came into the Alibi looking for her. She told everyone that he was stalking her, he was abusive, and she had a restraining order against him in Utah.

I told her she could stay at my place. That same night she asked if she could put my address on a restraining order that she was filing against him online and I consented.

However...

  • This email from the Fifth District Court in St. George, Utah which has jurisdiction in the town of Springdale where they were living proves she didn’t have a restraining order against him in Utah.
  • And this letter from the Multnomah County Circuit Court Records Clerk proves she didn’t file a restraining order against him here.

Granted, she may have started to file one here and then changed her mind. However, she told me many things about him when we first got together which I found out after the fact turned out to be a complete fabrication.

Here is her past revealed

Conclusio

Having related all that unflattering information about her, to balance the scales, I should add in all fairness that I AM NOT PERFECT.

I experience sub-optimum mental phenomena on a regular basis with its attendant physiological manifestations. In other words, I’ve developed a tick. Some of my co-workers who’ve inadvertently witnessed it probably think I have mental problems. But who doesn’t? I’m organized and conscientious, so fuck ‘em.

Also, I have a temper, bad breath, and I fart a lot. Kudos to her for putting up with me for so long. But just because I have a temper doesn't mean I'm violent. If I were, my criminal record would reflect that.

Also, the first seven months G and I were together were amazing. We were madly in love. We spent every day together with few exceptions. She was my best friend. We had a lot of good times and a lot of laughs–even up to the end.

I will also add that I learned a lesson.

I saw a red flag seven months in but I chose to ignore it because I didn't want my rent to go up and I had grown accustomed to regular sex.

(Incidentally, I received more than one I-told-you-so from friends and co-workers after the fact; in hindsight, all I can say is that my reason was eclipsed by her beauty.)

The lesson I learned is when you see a red flag either run, confront it, or break it off. However, if I had broken it off at the time, I wouldn't know what I know now which is that she seems to have a pattern of being an abusive, manipulative, vindictive liar.

But that's just my opinion.

Hic Non Est Finis
