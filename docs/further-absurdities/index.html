Further Absurdities

The Bass Guitar

In April 2025, someone I know told me that G sued me in small claims court because I stole a pair of her pants, her jacket, and her bass guitar. I found this out the same day the court entered the judgment against her.

The irony of her statement is on a par with the absurdity, because the person she told is someone:

  • Whom I've known for several years prior to meeting G;
  • Who met G through me at the Yamhill Pub;
  • Who was my supervisor when I introduced them;
  • Who was still my supervisor when she made the statement; and
  • Who already knew that I sued her when she said it.

As if theft and slander weren't enough, she apparently also felt the need to plagiarize my life. Well, it was a chuckleworthy attempt at best.

The trajectory of her pants I've already documented; the bass guitar is another story; and all stories need a setting or at least a context, so for that sake...

A hypothetical

If one heard that Harry met Sally, and Sally sued Harry because Harry stole Sally's bass guitar (among other things), one would make certain assumptions.

Namely, that

  • Sally broke up with Harry because he was a shithead;
  • Sally either owned the guitar before they were a couple, or acquired it while they were a couple; and
  • Harry took the guitar wrongfully.

If that's all one knew about their relationship, one would likely conclude that Harry should be the object of ill will and subject to punishment whether social, legal, corporal, or capital.

If that's all one knew, that is.

Well, like it or not, there are always two sides to every controversy.

A real world example

Thursday April 20 2023, I snapped at G over the phone while she was at work. I don't remember why exactly (probably hangover impatience), but I felt guilty enough to go to Guitar Center that same day and buy her a bass guitar, a guitar strap, and a guitar stand. (Well, to be honest, I bought us a bass guitar.) She swooned when she discovered it after work and great sex ensued.

Over the course of a year and a half, particularly on the downhill slope of our relationship, it became just another piece of furniture in our bedroom, another dusty relic of stale romance, which is why, I'm quite certain, she gave it away along with the rest of my belongings, because she never played it and she didn't want any reminders of my generosity.

In any case, it was a gift and I had no intention of taking it when I moved out regardless of the circumstances of our breakup.

The jacket, I would imagine, exited the apartment in the same fashion.

Her pants, however, can light themselves on fire and jump off a bridge.

Dysmorphia

In January 2025, I received word from a credible source that G came into the Nite Hawk and boasted about just getting off a plane from a tour with a band called Dysmorphia; then made out with some guy at the bar, and left alone without paying her tab.

In November 2025, I received word from another credible source that G bought a house in North Portland and went on a European tour with a band called Dysmorphia.

These are all facts of a fine and dandy nature if it weren't for other facts of a contradictory nature such as:

  • The Multnomah County Records and Archives Division shows there's no record of her buying a house in 2025 or any other year; and
  • A cursory Internet search shows there are only two bands from the US called Dysmorphia: one from Dallas, Texas and one from Erie, Pennsylvania, both of which I contacted and neither one has ever been to Europe.

Some people have body dysmorphia; I think G has reality dysmorphia.

Bad Reputation

While we were in the honeymoon phase of our relationship, she told me many stories about her past including one where her father was in a band called Bad Reputation from LA in the late 80s/early 90s and he brought her to a music studio when she was six to eight years old (circa 1992-94) where his band was recording and he introduced her to Ozzy Ozbourne and she sat on Ozzy's lap.

A cute and endearing story that would make any metalhead like her even more—it worked on me.

However,

  • A search for Bad Reputation on Encyclopaedia Metallum shows there was no such band;
  • A search for Bad Reputation on Discogs shows there was no such band;
  • Wikipedia's List of Bands from Los Angeles also shows there was no such band;
  • And this conversation with an employee at KROQ also shows there was no such band.

So, unless she mis-remembered her own father's band name (or I possibly did), her dad was never in a band called Bad Reputation which means she never sat on Ozzy's lap.

Maybe she watched the video for Crazy Babies when she was a kid and in that moment chose her life path. Oddly enough, she resembles one of the kids in the video, so I wouldn't be surprised.

I did something similar when I was her age while listening to Fandango by ZZ Top on my step-dad's 8-track player in Estacada, Oregon—neither of which amounted to anything but a court case and this long-winded diatribe.

Of all the band names to lie about, though, she sure picked an appropriate one.

Bad Repuation indeed.
