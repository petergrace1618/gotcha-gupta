Further Absurdities

The following are minor footnotes to the incident. (Please forgive my clunky verbiage; I know no other way.)

The Bass Guitar

The same day the judgment was entered I found out G told someone I know that she sued me in small claims court because I stole a pair of her pants, her jacket, and her bass guitar.

The irony of her statement is on a par with the absurdity because the person she told is:

  • Someone I've know for years prior to meeting G;
  • Someone who met G through me at the Yamhill Pub;
  • Someone who was my supervisor when I introduced them;
  • Someone who was my supervisor when she made the statement; and
  • Someone who already knew that I sued her when she said it.

Try to stifle a chuckle after that.

The trajectory of her pants I've already documented; the bass guitar is another story.

But first...

A hypothetical

If one hears that Harry and Sally were a couple, and Sally sued Harry because Harry stole Sally's guitar (among other things), one would make certain assumptions.

Namely, that

  • Sally broke up with Harry (probably because Harry was a shithead);
  • Sally either owned the guitar before they got together, or aquired it while they were together;
  • And Harry took it wrongfully.

If that's all one knew about the situation, one would likely conclude that Harry should be the object of ill will and subject to punishment whether social, legal, corporal, or capital.

If, that is, that's all one knew...

But, like it or not, there are always two sides to every controversy.

A real world example

On April 20 2023, I snapped at G over the phone while she was at work. I don't remember why exactly (probably hangover impatience), but I felt guilty enough to go to Guitar Center that same day and buy her a bass guitar, a guitar strap, and a guitar stand. (Well, to be honest, I bought us a bass guitar.) She swooned when she discovered it after work and great sex ensued.

Over the course of a year and a half, particularly on the downhill slope of our relationship, it became just another piece of furniture in our bedroom, another dusty relic of stale romance, which is why, I'm quite certain, she gave it away along with the rest of my belongings, because she never played it and she didn't want any reminders of my generosity.

In any case, it was a gift and I had no intention of taking it when I moved out regardless of the circumstances of our breakup. The jacket, I would imagine, exited the apartment in the same fashion. She had several, one of which I wore occassionally. Again, no reminders.

But fuck her pants. Catharsis is its own reward.

Dysmorphia

In January 2025 (two months after the incident), an ex-coworker told me that G came into her work, boasted about just getting off a plane after a tour with a band called Dysmorphia, then left without paying her tab.

In November 2025, a friend told me that G bought a house in North Portland, and went on a European tour with a band called Dysmorphia.

These are all facts of a fine and dandy nature if it weren't for other facts of a contradictory nature. For example...

The Multnomah County Records and Archives Division website shows there's no record of her buying a house in 2025 or any other year.

There are seven metal bands called Dysmorphia around the world but only one from the US in Dallas, Texas; and there's a Riot Grrrl band band called Dysmorphia from Erie, Pennsylvania both of which I contacted and neither one has done a European tour.

Some people have body dysmorphia. I think G has reality dysmorphia.

Bad Reputation

While we were in the honeymoon phase of our relationship, she told me that her father, Yuli Ruiz, was in a band called Bad Reputation in the 80s and/or 90s, and one time when she was a kid (six or eight years old circa 1992-94) he brought her to a music studio where his band was recording and she met Ozzy Ozbourne and sat on his lap.

A cute and endearing story that would make any metalhead like her even more. (It worked on me.)

However,

  • A search for Bad Reputation on metal-archives.com produces no results;
  • A search for Bad Reputation on discogs shows that there was no such LA band from the 80s or 90s;
  • The List of Bands from Los Angeles Wikipedia page shows there was no such band;
  • And this conversation with a DJ at KROQ, the world famous LA radio station, also shows there was no such band.

Unless she mis-remembered the name or I mis-remembered her telling me the name (which I think is unlikely), her dad wasn't in a band called Bad Reputation which means she never met Ozzy Ozbourne.

Of all the band names to make up, she sure picked an appropriate one.

Bad Repuation indeed.
