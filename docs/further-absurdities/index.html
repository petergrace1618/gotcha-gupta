Further Absurdities

The following are minor yet mirthful footnotes to the incident.

The Bass Guitar

Someone I know told me that G sued me in small claims court because I stole a pair of her pants, her jacket, and her bass guitar. That same night I found out the court entered the judgment against her.

The consequent irony of her statement is on a par with the absurdity because, like her other slander, the person she told is

  • Someone I've known for years prior to meeting G;
  • Someone G met through me at the Yamhill Pub;
  • Someone who was my supervisor when I introduced them;
  • Someone who was still my supervisor when she made the statement;
  • Someone who already knew that I sued her; and
  • Someone she should have known I was still in contact with when she said it.

As if theft and slander weren't enough, she decided to plagiarize my life. It was a chuckleworthy attempt at best.

The trajectory of her pants I've already documented; the bass guitar is another story; and all stories need a setting (or at least a context). For that sake, here is...

A hypothetical

If one heard that Harry and Sally were a couple, then Sally sued Harry because Harry stole Sally's guitar (among other things), one would make certain assumptions.

Namely, that

  • Sally broke up with Harry (probably because Harry was a shithead);
  • Sally either owned the guitar before they were c couple, or aquired it while they were a couple; and
  • Harry took it wrongfully.

If that's all one knew about their relationship, one would likely conclude that Harry should be the object of ill will and subject to punishment whether social, legal, corporal, or capital.

If that's all one knew, that is.

But, like it or not, there are always two sides to every controversy.

A real world example

On April 20 2023, I snapped at G over the phone while she was at work. I don't remember why exactly (probably hangover impatience), but I felt guilty enough to go to Guitar Center that same day and buy her a bass guitar, a guitar strap, and a guitar stand. (Well, to be honest, I bought us a bass guitar.) She swooned when she discovered it after work and great sex ensued.

Over the course of a year and a half, particularly on the downhill slope of our relationship, it became just another piece of furniture in our bedroom, another dusty relic of stale romance, which is why, I'm quite certain, she gave it away along with the rest of my belongings; because she never played it and she didn't want any reminders of my generosity.

In any case, it was a gift and I had no intention of taking it when I moved out regardless of the circumstances of our breakup.

The jacket, I would imagine, exited the apartment in the same fashion.

Her pants, however, can go jump off a bridge.

Dysmorphia, My Ass

In January 2025, two months after the incident, an ex-coworker told me that G came into the Nite Hawk and boasted about just getting off a plane from a tour with a band called Dysmorphia, and then made out with some guy at the bar, then left without paying her tab.

In November 2025, a year after the incident, an old friend told me that G bought a house in North Portland and went on a European tour with a band called Dysmorphia.

These are all facts of a fine and dandy nature if it weren't for other facts of a contradictory nature. For example,

  1. The Multnomah County Records and Archives Division shows there's no record of her buying a house in 2025 or any other year; and
  2. A cursory Internet search shows there are only two bands from the US called Dysmorphia: one from Dallas, Texas and one from Erie, Pennsylvania, both of which I contacted and neither one has ever been to Europe.

Some people have body dysmorphia; I think G has reality dysmorphia.

Bad Reputation

While we were in the honeymoon phase of our relationship, she told me that her father, Yuli Ruiz, was in a band called Bad Reputation from LA in the 90s, and one time when she was a kid (six or eight years old circa 1992-94), he brought her to a music studio where his band was recording, and she sat on Ozzy Ozbourne's lap.

A cute and endearing story that would make any metalhead like her even more. (It worked on me.)

However,

  • A search for Bad Reputation on Encyclopaedia Metallum shows there was no such band;
  • A search for Bad Reputation on Discogs shows there was no such band;
  • Wikipedia's List of Bands from Los Angeles also shows there was no such band;
  • And this conversation I had with an employee at KROQ, the world famous LA radio station, also shows there was no such band.

So, unless she or I mis-remembered her own father's band name (which I think is unlikely), her dad was never in a band called Bad Reputation which means she never sat on Ozzy Ozbourne's lap.

(Maybe she saw the video for Crazy Babies when she was a kid and chose her life path in that moment—I've seen it many times throughout my life, but now it has a different meaning.)

Of all the band names to lie about, she sure picked an appropriate one.

Bad Repuation indeed.
