Further Absurdities

The following are mere footnotes to the incident.

Her Bass Guitar

On or about Wednesday April 30 2025, one of my supervisors whom I had been aquainted with for several years prior to meeting G and whom G met through me at the Yamhill Pub, told me that G a few days prior told him that she sued me in small claims court because I stole a pair of her pants, her jacket, and her bass guitar.

The absurdity of her statement is on a par with the irony considering that it was the same day that the judgment against her was entered.

The trajectory of her pants I've already documented; the bass guitar is another story.

A hypothetical

If one hears that Harry and Sally were a couple, and Sally sued Harry because Harry stole Sally's guitar (among other things), one would make certain assumptions.

Namely, that

  • Sally broke up with Harry (probably because Harry was a shithead);
  • Sally either owned the guitar before they got together, or aquired it while they were together;
  • And Harry took it wrongfully.

If that's all one knew about the situation, one would be justified in coming to the conclusion that Harry should be the object of ill will and subject to punishment whether social, legal, corporal, or capital.

If, that is, that's all one knew...

But, like it or not, there are always two sides to every controversy.

A real world example

On April 20 2023, I snapped at G over the phone while she was at work. I don't remember why exactly (probably hangover impatience), but I felt guilty enough to go to Guitar Center that same day and buy her a bass guitar, a guitar strap, and a guitar stand. (Well, to be honest, I bought us a bass guitar.) She swooned when she got home from work and discovered it. Great sex ensued.

Over the course of a year and a half—particularly on the downhill slope of our relationship—it became just another piece of furniture in our bedroom—another dusty relic of stale romance—which is why, I'm quite certain, she gave it away along with the rest of my belongings because she never played it and she didn't want any reminders of my generosity.

In any case, it was a gift and I had no intention of taking it when I moved out regardless of the circumstances of our breakup. The jacket, I would imagine, exited the apartment in the same fashion. She had several, one of which I wore occassionally. Again, no reminders.

But fuck her pants.

Dysmorphia My Ass

In January 2025 (approximately two months after the incident), an ex-coworker whom I've known for several years prior to meeting G and who met G through me told me that G came into her work and boasted about just getting off a plane from a tour with a band called Dysmorphia and then left without paying her tab.

In November 2025 (approximately a year after "the incident"), another friend that I've known for years prior to meeting G and who met G through me told me that G bought a house in North Portland and went on a European tour with a band called Dysmorphia.

These are all facts of a fine and dandy nature if it weren't for other facts of a contradictory nature. For example...

A search for her name on Multnomah County's Records and Archives Division website shows there's no record of her buying a house.

And according to my cursory research on Metal-Archives, Instagram, and Facebook, there are eight bands called Dysmorphia around the globe, but only two from the states. One is a death metal band from Dallas, Texas; the other is a Riot Grrrl band from Erie, Pennsylvania.

Both bands are active and consistently post flyers for their shows and tours on social media, but there's no evidence that either band did an American tour or a European tour at the time she claims.

As if the coffin nails weren't fully hammered (call it an obsession if you want, I don't care), I contacted the touring manager of Dallas Dysmorphia, and whomever manages Erie Dysmorphia's Facebook page to corroboratingly negative results.

Some people have body dysmorphia. I think she has reality dysmorphia.

Bad Reputation

While we were in the honeymoon phase of our relationship, she told me that her father, Yuli Ruiz, was in a band called Bad Reputation in the 80s and/or 90s, and one time when she was a kid (six or eight years old circa 1992-94) he brought her to a music studio where his band was recording and she met Ozzy Ozbourne and sat on his lap.

A cute and endearing story that would make any metalhead like her even more. (It worked on me.)

However,

  • A search for Bad Reputation on metal-archives.com produces no results;
  • A search for Bad Reputation on discogs shows that there was no such band from LA in the 80s or 90s;
  • The List of Bands from Los Angeles page on Wikipedia shows there was no such band;
  • And this conversation I had on LinkedIn with a DJ at the world famous LA radio station, KROQ, also shows there was no such band.

Unless she mis-remembered the name or I mis-remembered her telling me the name (which I think is unlikely), her dad wasn't in a band called Bad Reputation which means she never met Ozzy Ozbourne.

Of all the band names to make up, she sure picked an appropriate one.

Bad Repuation indeed.
