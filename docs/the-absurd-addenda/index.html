The Absurd Addenda

The dress isn't hers

On the first trip to return her belongings, I returned a dress which she later claimed wasn't hers.

This is a rather silly thing to deny considering that I have a picture of her wearing that same dress at my niece's high school graduation.

She wants her shit back

She threatened to remove my belongings from the apartment, then later that night let people into the apartment whom she claimed were friends of mine, gave them a bunch of my stuff, then two weeks after that claimed that they stole her shit.

How exactly did that happen? Did she give my stuff away then pass out on the toilet while they were still there?

And exactly what shit does she want back? According to my friend, she said she sued me in small claims court over a pair of pants, a jacket, and a bass guitar.

Could it be the same pants I already returned that she hid in a kitchen cabinet next to two of my shirts? Seems plausible.

The bass guitar, on the other hand, I bought for her, and I even have a copy of the receipt.

It also seems plausible that she gave the bass guitar away with my stuff because she never played it, and she didn't want any reminders of my generosity after ripping me off.

She doesn't know who packed my clothes

She said she was going to put all my stuff on the porch and to come and get it; and the very next day a duffel bag of my clothes was on the porch, the rest of my clothes were in hampers in the living room, and two of my shirts were in a kitchen cabinet next to a pair of her own pants.

Then later she claims that she didn't pack my clothes, doesn't know who did, and doesn't know how my shirts got into the kitchen cabinet.

How could she not know? Here are a few scenarios in order from reasonable to ridiculous.

  • She did it herself; but, for reasons known only to her and her fairy godmother, she chooses to deny it.
  • She invited a complete stranger to the apartment or had someone send a complete stranger to the apartment whom she directed to pack my clothes, thereby giving herself plausible deniability to claim that she didn’t do it and doesn't know who did.
  • Someone broke into the apartment, didn’t steal or break anything, but decided to take all my clothes—and my clothes alone—put them in two hampers and a duffel bag; then as an afterthought, arbitrarily picked out two shirts and a pair of pants, hid them in a kitchen cabinet, then left the duffel bag on the porch after locking the door behind them.
  • A roaming poltergeist, powerful yet surprisingly organized and selective, blew through the neighborhood looking to cause mayhem; descended upon the apartment; teleported my clothes from off the hangers and off the shelf into two hampers and a duffel bag; and, in a final act of mischief, apported through solid matter two of my favorite shirts, a pair of her pants, and the duffel bag of clothes before returning to the Alibi to repose and snicker.

Oh, the absurdity.
