Post Incidentum

She called me on Friday December 27 2024. The only reason I answered was because I thought she might be calling to apologize for slandering me and calling me a psycho ten days prior.

I was wrong.

The Context

Sunday November 3, the day after the incident, I came to the apartment to pick up some essentials so I could get through my work week and move out the following weekend.

She showed up while I was packing. She told me to leave and come back tomorrow when she was at work, and then tried to push me out of the bedroom.

I got fed up with the confontation, so I asked her if I could trust her to leave my stuff undisturbed until the next day when I could move out. I had to ask her twice before she agreed. Matty witnessed the whole interaction.

That night when I was back at Leslie’s, she called and said she was going to put all my stuff on the porch and to come get it. I told her not to do it and reminded her that she agreed to leave my things undisturbed until the next day. She said she was going to do it anyway. I told her she was being completely unreasonable. She said “No, YOU'RE being unreasonable!”

Afterwards, Leslie and Matty convinced me that she probably just wanted me to come back to the apartment, and wasn’t actually going to throw my stuff out. She then called me ten times in a row, and I ignored every call.

The next day when I came to the apartment to move out (aside from the aforementioned belongings that were missing) a duffel bag of my clothes was on the porch, and a bunch of my things were stacked up in the living room including the rest of my clothes which were in two hampers.

—

Over the following month while I was getting settled in at Leslie’s, I discovered that some of her belongings were mixed in with mine. I considered throwing them away, but I didn’t want to give her any excuse to accuse me of stealing her stuff, so I made several trips to return it.

The First Trip

Friday November 15, I returned a dress and a few other things to the porch of the apartment and then went to work. Afterwards, she sent me a text message saying that the dress wasn’t hers, to give it back to my "friend", and that she asked for her pants back. I told her I wanted my guitars and my comic books back. She told me to ask my “friends” and to tell them she wants her shit back, too.

The Second Trip

Sunday December 1, I came to the apartment to drop off the key, to return another one of her shirts, and to make a final attempt to look for my bag of kitchen utensils. I couldn't find them, but while I was looking I found a mass of black clothes in the back of a kitchen cabinet. When I pulled it out, I discovered that it was two of my favorite shirts. I had a WTF? moment, and resignedly stuffed them in my backpack. On my way out, she came out of the bedroom. I presented her Dot's Cafe shirt to her, set it on a chair, and then left. When I got home, I dumped out my backpack, and discovered that in addition to my shirts was also a pair of her own pants.

The Third Trip

Friday December 6, I returned her pants to the porch of the apartment along with a few other things that I discovered in the intervening week. After I left, I told her “I found your pants. They were in the kitchen next to my shirts. You had them the whole time.”

She didn’t respond.

—

Altogether, I returned the following things of hers:

  • A pair of her pants,
  • A dress,
  • Two shirts,
  • A CD,
  • A Discman,
  • A poster,
  • A book, and
  • A beer koozie.

And that was that.

Or so I thought.

The Slander

Tuesday December 17 2024, I went to the Happy House by our former apartment. The bartender, whom I’ve known for several years prior to G and who befriended G while we were together, told me G said that I ripped her off, that I was on meth, and that I came to the apartment one night to steal more of her stuff but she was home so I left.

I was dumbfounded.

After this disclosure I went to the bathroom. When I came out, G’s coke dealer was sitting a few bar stools down from mine. We exchanged greetings. (He's an amicable fellow.) A few moments later, G appeared from the bathroom and stood next to him. In an attempt to defuse the tangible awkwardness, the bartender said to G, “Why don’t you two go on the patio and work it out?”

G responded “I don’t want to talk to that psycho!”

I just laughed and left shortly thereafter.

The Pretense

Ten days later, she called me and said someone told her I wasn’t doing well and she should check on me. (I don't remember the exact chronology, but what follows are the memories that persist.)

I asked her who told her that. She said “I don't want to give up their name.“

I said “I just want my stuff back.” She said “So do I!”

I reminded her that she already admitted giving my stuff away in several text messages and a voicemail. She again denied it.

I told her that her text messages were part of a police report. She said she filed a police report, too.

She mentioned that she wanted her pants back. I told her I already gave her pants back.

She said she was talking about her “other” pants. I reminded her that I found her pants in a kitchen cabinet next to two of my shirts. She said she didn’t know how they got there.

I said, “You packed my clothes.”
She said, “No, I didn’t!”
I said, “Then who did?”
She said, “I don’t know!”

The last thing I said to her was “You’re a liar. Don’t talk to me anymore,” and then hung up on her.

She immediately called me back twice. I didn’t answer.

She then sent me a series of text messages which, as I stated in the beginning, perfectly exemplifies her disingenuous behavior.

The only reason I called you was because someone said that you weren't well and asked me to check in on you

Also, I guess I can tell you that your police report is considered fraud because my job was able to prove that I was at work when you called

Just letting you know. Give me back my shit, please, thank you, go fuck yourself.

I asked her what the case number of her police report is.

69420. Go fuck yourself

I had to search for the info so you can do it yourself

Also, you were on speaker phone.....with my lawyer

/paralegal

Talk to you later. He's saying to expect a letter in the mail

In lieu of accolades for her admirable attempt at an honorary Florence Nightingale award, I offer...

The Absurdity

She gave away a bunch of my stuff in the middle of the night to some people she claimed were my friends without any confirmation from me, then two weeks later she claimed they stole her shit.

—

She claimed that her job can prove that she was at work when I called even though my call history from that night clearly shows that I didn't call her once, whereas she called me a multitude of times.

—

She said the dress I returned wasn't hers despite the fact that I have a picture of her wearing that dress at my niece's high school graduation.

—

She told me that my police report is considered fraud, then proceeds to tell me that the case number of hers is 69-420, then tells me to go fuck myself.

—

She hid two of my favorite shirts in a kitchen cabinet along with a pair of her own pants that she later claimed she wants back even though I made several trips to return her belongings after she gave away a bunch of mine.

Then she turns around and tells somebody I know that I ripped her off and I’m on meth.

Then she shows up at the bar with her coke dealer.

Then she calls me a psycho.

—

She said she doesn't know who packed my clothes even though she threatened to throw my stuff on the porch, and the very next day a duffel bag of my clothes was on the porch.

Well, here are a few scenarios that might explain how she wouldn't know who packed my clothes (leaving out the obvious which is that she did it herself but denies it for reasons known only to herself and her fairy godmother).

  • She invited a complete stranger to the apartment or had someone send a complete stranger to the apartment whom she directed to pack my clothes, thereby giving herself plausible deniability to claim that she didn’t do it and doesn't know who did.
  • Someone broke into the apartment, didn’t steal or break anything, but decided to put all my clothes in two hampers and a duffel bag; and, in a final fuck-you maneuver, arbitrarily picked out two of my favorite shirts and a pair of her pants, hid them in a kitchen cabinet, then dropped the duffel bag on the porch, and absconded after locking the door behind themself.
  • A roaming poltergeist, powerful yet surprisingly organized and selective, blew through the neighborhood looking to cause mayhem; descended upon the apartment; teleported my clothes from off the hangers and off the shelf into two hampers and a duffel bag; and, in a final act of mischief, apported through solid matter two of my favorite shirts, a pair of her pants, and the duffel bag of clothes before returning to the Alibi to repose and snicker.

Any of these are more plausible than her feigned ignorance.

—

Lastly but not leastly, not only did she call me claiming to check on my well-being then tell me to go fuck myself twice, she also qualified her statement in writing by saying that the only reason she called me was to check on my well-being.

If that were the case, then why would I be on speaker phone with her “lawyer/paralegal?”

Also, who talks to their lawyer at 10:09 PM on a Friday night?

Also, it’s been over a year as of this writing and I still haven’t received any letter from a lawyer or a paralegal.

At the very least, it’s nice to know that she genuinely cares about my well-being.
