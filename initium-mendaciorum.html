Initium Mendaciorum

After the incident and her subsequent slander, it occurred to me that if she did that to me, she may have done something similar to her ex-boyfriend (hereinafter E), and fled Utah to escape accountability.

To satisfy my morbid curiosity, I contacted one of his family members—a cousin who I will refer to as M, a lovely woman who said she grew up very close to E who was, in her own words, "like my little brother".

She did however, give me permission to publish our email exchange, so I'll summarize her account which turned out to be a completely different story than the one G related to me.

To demonstrate the contrast...

My Recollection of G’s Version

They met at the Slabtown New Seasons as co-workers. She was staying on the couch at some creepy old guy's place who tried to put the moves on her. E offered to let her stay at his place. They became romantically involved. After some time, they decided to move to Springdale, Utah where he was from. He moved there first to get settled and line up a job for both of them. She followed several months later. They worked at a brewery near Zion National Park. She enjoyed working there, but he drank a lot, got fired, and she had to support both of them. His drinking continued, and he became abusive. One night, he threw a burrito at her while she was lying in bed. Another night, he left the house with a friend to go out drinking, and she used the opportunity to quickly pack up and leave. She took the car and headed back to Portland. She ran out of gas along the way, and had to ask someone to send her money. When she got to Portland, she stayed on a female friend's couch somewhere on Interstate Ave. Her friend had a cat and she didn't like having cat hair on her black clothes. She applied at the Alibi and the kitchen manager hired her no questions asked because they happened to be ex-coworkers. E tracked her down from Utah and confronted her on the patio of the Alibi. She told her co-workers that her abusive ex-boyfriend was stalking her and she had a restraining order against him in Utah. She told me afterwards that he must have put a tracking app on her phone while they were still in Utah.

An unfortunate story, and one that would make anyone sympathize with her.

And now...

My Summary of M's version

They met at New Seasons. He had just gotten out of rehab a month prior. She complained about her bad roommate situation: “a stalkery, abusive roommate story.” He offered her a safe place to crash. They became romantically involved, and she never moved out. Her presence caused tension with his roommate.

He felt suffocated because they lived together and worked together. He tried changing shifts and changing jobs but she followed him every time. They fought about it, and he began drinking again.

E asked M to take him to rehab while G was at work. When M picked him up he had two black eyes. He didn’t remember what happened but G insisted that he’d deserved it. He hoped she would be gone when he got out, but she was still living there when he did. They reconciled briefly. She convinced him that rehab had been a mistake.

Six months later, his sister flew to Portland to take him to medical detox. He changed his phone number so G couldn’t contact him, and they left for Utah.

In Springdale he began “sorting out the mindfuck that Gyps had been.” Six months later G showed up at his mom’s house, camped outside for days, and eventually charmed her way back into his life. E helped her get a job, but she got fired from three places.

She barricaded herself in the bedroom, and made him sleep on the couch. Their roommates broke up with her on his behalf, and told her she had to move out because “she was making everyone miserable & wasn’t contributing rent.”

E drove her to the Las Vegas airport so she could go to Colorado for a job she had lined up. She came back less than 48 hours later. They tried to stay at one of his aunts' house in Vegas, but she was recovering from a stroke and the family didn’t want G around her. So, they sold his phone for gas money and drove to Portland.

She got hired at the Alibi shortly after. Meanwhile, they were living in his car and trying to figure out their next step.

On April 23 2023, he took his own life.

R.I.P., E.R.L.

Comparison

Obviously, the two versions are vastly different. One is purportedly a first hand account while the other is second hand. But which one is more accurate?

Well, here's some evidence that corroborates M’s version:

Her presence caused tension with his roommate.

According to this police report, in February 2020, G moved in with E and his roommate. G and the roommate had a physical altercation during an argument about the roommate using their bathroom. Granted, the roommate got arrested for assault, but this happened a mere two weeks after G moved in.

She blamed him & punished him because things weren't going well for her.

According to this police report, in May 2020, an officer was dispatched to G and E’s house again for disorderly conduct. When the officer arrived he saw “a female climbing off of a male in the living room area.” G told the officer that “she wants her boyfriend out of the house” because “he doesn't work and she just lost her job.” The officer also noticed multiple scratches on E’s neck and shoulders.

My own experience with G.

In September 2023, she got fired from the Alibi for drinking on the job. She felt she was wrongfully terminated even though she had been warned, had been on probation for a number of weeks, and then did it again. She criticized me repeatedly for months because I didn’t quit in protest despite the fact that she had only been there for seven months whereas I had been there for five and a half years.

Let the reader decide which version is more accurate.
